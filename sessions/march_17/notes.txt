Link to paper: https://aclanthology.org/2023.cl-4.2/

This paper introduces a structured framework to obtain human annotations for Natural Language Generation regarding their faithfulness to given sources &rarr; Attributable to Identified Sources (AIS)

What we liked:

- very structured approach
- presented framework and ablations can help other researchers on a budget as they can rely on a proven method

What we didn't like:

- task seems trivial
- very little insights
- definitions are very rigorous but this does not seem necessary
- the annotation procedure is only described very brielfy which also raises questions about ethics in working with the annotators
- esp. expert agreement is not that high which is not sufficiently discussed
- annotators guidelines are very long

What we can take for our own research:

- multi-step approach (first interpretability, then AIS) can easily be implemented
- insights on likert scale are very valuable
